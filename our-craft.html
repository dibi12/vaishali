The Philosophy

Cloth as a carrier of culture, history, and the human hand.

For Vaishali Shadangule, craft is not embellishment — it is the foundation. Every collection begins not with a sketch, but with the loom. Over 18 years, she has travelled India's textile heartlands, building relationships with weavers and artisan communities whose knowledge stretches back generations.

The result is a body of work that positions Indian handwoven textiles not as heritage objects but as the material of contemporary couture — on equal footing with the finest European fabrics, and presented on the world's highest stage: the Paris Haute Couture calendar.

The Communities

12+ Weaving Communities
Across India

Each collection is built around a specific textile region. Vaishali works directly with master weavers, bypassing intermediaries to ensure fair compensation, creative collaboration, and the preservation of techniques at risk of being lost.

West Bengal

Murshidabad Silk

Fine mulberry silk handwoven on traditional pit looms. Known for its luminous drape and extraordinary lightness — the defining textile of the Kintsugi collection.

Uttar Pradesh

Varanasi Zari

Gold and silver brocade weavers whose craft traces to Mughal court traditions. Each metre of zari fabric can take a master weaver up to three weeks to complete.

Madhya Pradesh

Maheshwar Cotton

Handwoven cotton and silk blends from the Narmada riverbanks. Distinguished by their reversible weave, subtle border patterns, and natural dye heritage.

Madhya Pradesh

Chanderi Natural Fibre

Ultra-sheer handwoven fabric blending silk and cotton, originally commissioned for Mughal royalty. Its translucency and gossamer weight make it ideal for layered couture silhouettes.

Andhra Pradesh

Pochampally Ikat

Resist-dyed warp-and-weft ikat with geometric precision. The dyeing and weaving process is inseparable — pattern emerges only when the threads align perfectly on the loom.

Rajasthan

Kota Doria

Exceptionally fine cotton-silk check weave from Kota district. The square check (khat) motif is woven with such precision it requires years of training to master.

Odisha

Sambalpuri Ikat

Tribal textile tradition using vegetable dyes and bandha (tie-dye) resist technique. Patterns carry cosmological symbolism — lotus, conch shell, temple spire — woven into the cloth itself.

Tamil Nadu

Kanchipuram Silk

Heavy mulberry silk with zari borders, woven on a three-shuttle loom. The body, border, and pallu (end panel) are woven separately then joined — a technique unique to Kanchipuram.

How We Work

From loom to look.

Each garment begins with the weaver, not the designer.

01

Community Research

Vaishali visits textile communities in person — studying local techniques, understanding the constraints of each loom, and listening to what the weavers want to create. No brief is issued before this phase.

02

Co-Creation

Design emerges through dialogue. Weavers propose thread combinations, densities, and border motifs. Vaishali develops the silhouette around what the fabric wants to be — not what the sketch demands.

03

Handweaving

Every metre is woven by hand. Production timelines are dictated by the weaver's pace and the complexity of the structure. We do not accelerate this process. Some fabrics take months to produce.

04

Atelier Construction

Fabric arrives at our Mumbai atelier, where master tailors and embroiderers interpret Vaishali's construction sketches. Each garment is assembled by hand, with zero machine sewing on couture pieces.

05

Finishing & Quality

Every finished garment is inspected against a 40-point quality checklist. Hand-finishing — edge-rolling, French seams, interior embroidery — is completed before a single garment leaves the atelier.

06

The World Stage

Completed collections travel to Paris, where they are presented at Haute Couture Week — making Vaishali S the first Indian label on the official Paris couture calendar. The craft of India, on the global stage.

I design from observation, not imagination. The cloth teaches me what it wants to become.

— Vaishali Shadangule, Founder & Creative Director
